Study on Mediterranean Diet

Author: Prof. Michael Stone

The Mediterranean diet emphasizes vegetables, fruits, nuts, legumes, whole grains, fish, and olive oil. It is widely recognized for promoting cardiovascular health and longevity.

Research indicates that people following this diet have lower levels of inflammation, reduced risk of heart disease, and improved metabolic profiles. This diet also includes moderate amounts of dairy and lean protein, providing balanced nutrition.

Adopting a Mediterranean diet can improve gut health, support brain function, and aid in weight management. It is sustainable and adaptable, making it suitable for long-term lifestyle changes.
